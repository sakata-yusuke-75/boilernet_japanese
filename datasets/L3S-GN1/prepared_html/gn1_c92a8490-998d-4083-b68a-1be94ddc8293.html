Gainey gives Canadiens good mid-season grades, except on faceoffs

at 12:19 on January 7, 2008, EST.



THE CANADIAN PRESS

MONTREAL - General manager Bob Gainey gives the Montreal Canadiens a passing grade for the first half of the NHL season nearly everywhere except in the faceoff circle.

Gainey said that unless his defensive centres start winning more key draws, he may have to deal for someone who can before the trading deadline in February.

"We can't finish games with 30 or 35 per cent of faceoffs won," Gainey said Monday. "If the players here can't solve it, I'll look at adding or replacing a player here with one who can win important faceoffs."

The Canadiens reached the mid-point of the season in fifth place in the Eastern Conference with a 20-13-8 record.

They were three points better at the same point last season, but then went into a swoon that saw them miss the playoffs.

Gainey said this year's team is better because it takes fewer penalties and is stronger playing at even strength.

An oddity is that the Canadiens have been better on the road, where they are 13-6-3. Montreal is 7-7-5 at home.

"I have a belief that you can fix a home record easier than a road record,' he said. "The team that doesn't play well on the road is one that has some fear, some anxiety about playing in another team's arena".

"I feel better about our chances to improve the situation we have now."
