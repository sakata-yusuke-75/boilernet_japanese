Mowbray: Delighted with display

Mowbray salutes rampant Baggies

West Brom manager Tony Mowbray hailed the finishing power of his table-topping side after they crushed 10-man Scunthorpe 5-0 at The Hawthorns.

Kevin Phillips took his tally for the season to 15 with a double - before missing a penalty - and he was joined on the scoresheet by Robert Koren, Zoltan Gera and Craig Beattie.

Scunthorpe's fate was effectively sealed when Marcus Williams was sent off for bringing down Gera just eight minutes after Phillips had broken the deadlock.

Mowbray said: "There were some great individual goals. It was great finishing by Phillips, Gera's was an amazing finish and so was Beattie's.

"They were wonder goals. You almost had a goal of the season competition in one game. There was some pretty spectacular stuff out there.

"I enjoy watching good football and I thought we moved the ball around the pitch well. I enjoy winning. That is why we are here.

"There are no easy games in this division and you have to earn the right to win."

Mowbray admitted the visitors had made it difficult before Phillips opened the scoring.

"Early on this game was tight. It is a tactical battle as well as a physical confrontation.

"The longer it goes on and spreads out it becomes more of a football match.

"They played a shape that had potential to cause us problems but unfortunately that went out of the window after the sending off. That allowed us to get a hold of the game."

Mowbray defended his decision to take off Phillips two minutes after missing from the spot, denying him the chance of a hat-trick.

"Kevin came off because his job was done for the day," added Mowbray.

"He was unhappy that he missed a penalty because he had the opportunity score a hat-trick. That is football.

"My interest is the next game. If I had left him on and he got crocked in the last minute we could have missed him for six weeks. That would not be good management."

Albion's opening goal left Scunthorpe fuming as they were convinced Phillips was offside when he collected a long cross-field ball from James Morrison in the 35th minute.

Phillips volleyed his shot over Joe Murphy from the edge of the area - leading to a furious reaction from Scunthorpe boss Nigel Adkins, who was sent to the stands by referee Neil Swarbrick for taking his protests too far.

That was the beginning of the end for Scunthorpe who were reduced to 10 men eight minutes later when Williams was dismissed for pulling back Gera.

Albion swiftly took advantage with Koren sweeping home his second goal in two games from close range, converting a Carl Hoefkens cross at the second attempt after Ian Baraclough had blocked his first shot.

In-form Phillips then helped himself to a second in the 52nd minute when he chipped into an empty net from 16 yards after Murphy had saved from Morrison.

Phillips should have completed his hat-trick six minutes later when Murphy hauled down Ishmael Miller.

But his penalty was weak and Murphy dived low to his left to beat the ball away.

While Murphy was equal to the task from the spot he could not prevent Gera scoring with a 20-yard volley from the left hand edge of the area after 77 minutes.

Beattie then completed the rout from almost the identical spot nine minutes from time with a curling right foot shot.

Adkins felt that the game swung on Phillips' first goal and the dismissal of Williams.

He said: "For the first goal, in my opinion, and probably 25,000 people in the stadium the player was five yards offside.

"The linesman was closer to me than being up with play. He did not give the offside and that was very disappointing.

"I have then gone and stood five yards outside the technical area, not sworn or used any abusive language, and the referee and the fourth official have come over and asked me to go and sit in the stand.

"As for the sending off, I had a super view in the stand of the players colliding.

"I could clearly see the referee signal the players had collided and then the linesman, with his new Christmas present, put his flag up and then the referee came over.

"To be a red card was really damaging for us because it was going to be hard enough as it was against a top team with 11 men let alone 10.

"But let's not take anything away from West Brom. They will be a Premier League team next season and it is a great experience for me and my team to play against them."
