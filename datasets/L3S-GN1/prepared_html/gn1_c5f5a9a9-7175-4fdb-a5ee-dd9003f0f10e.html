North Korea

US urges N Korea to come clean

By Daniel Dombey in Washington

Published: December 31 2007 00:29 | Last updated: December 31 2007 00:29

The US on Sunday urged North Korea to come clean about the full range of its nuclear activities, as it became all but certain that the secretive communist state would break its promise to make such a declaration by the end of the year.

This is despite a series of conciliatory gestures by Washington in recent weeks, including a letter by President George W Bush to North Korea?s leader Kim Jong-il that began ?Dear Mr chairman?. The US has come to see the six party talks on Pyongyang?s nuclear programme as one of its latter day foreign policy successes.

Signalling possible difficulties in the talks, Washington is now increasingly concerned that North Korea is also dragging its feet in ?disabling? its Yongbyon reactor, which has produced plutonium for its small arsenal of nuclear bombs.

?It is unfortunate that North Korea has not yet met its commitments by providing a complete and correct declaration of its nuclear programs and slowing down the process of disablement,? said Tom Casey, a state department spokesman.

However, US officials have previously indicated that a North Korean declaration delayed by a few weeks might be preferable to one that was incomplete. The US is particularly keen to hear whether Pyongyang will admit to an extensive programme of uranium enrichment - an alternative to plutonium as a route to nuclear weapons.
