Nash a smash as Jackets double up Oilers

Columbus, OH (Sports Network) - Rick Nash registered his second career hat trick to help the Columbus Jackets double up the struggling Edmonton Oilers, 4-2, at Nationwide Arena.

Jiri Novotny had a goal and an assist and Nikolai Zherdev collected two helpers for the Blue Jackets, who have won two straight and three of four overall. Pascal Leclaire made 26 saves in the win.

"I told the players today the third period was the best we've played at home (so far this season)," said Columbus coach Ken Hitchcock. "I thought we did a great job managing the game in the third period. Both teams really competed tonight."

Ethan Moreau and Steve Staios tallied for Edmonton, which has dropped six straight. Moreau added an assist and Dwayne Roloson allowed three goals on 32 shots.

"You can't continually say you're not getting the breaks, we just lack the confidence to finish the game off and every night it seems to be something else that is our undoing," said Edmonton coach Craig MacTavish.

Nash's power-play goal broke a 2-2 tie with 3:21 remaining in the third and completed the hat trick with a empty-net goal in the waning seconds of regulation.

Novotny gave the Blue Jackets a 1-0 lead at 8:15 of the first. Roloson stopped Zherdev's shot from the left circle, but Novotny, driving hard to the net, chipped the rebound between the left arm and torso of Roloson.

Moreau drew the Oilers even when he collected a loose puck in the slot and buried it into an empty net at 7:23 of the second.

It was Moreau's first goal since October 17, 2006 after the winger missed the final 75 games of last season with a dislocated right shoulder and the first 38 games this season due to a fractured left foot suffered in the preseason.

Nash gave the Blue Jackets a 2-1 lead near the middle of the stanza with a short-handed tally.

Edmonton answered back with 6:42 remaining when Staios' blast from the right point whizzed underneath the right arm of Leclaire.

Game Notes

Edmonton had won three straight and four of the last five meetings entering play...Zherdev has registered two goals and seven points in his last three games...Columbus was 1-for-3 on the power play, while the Oilers failed to score on their four opportunities with the man advantage...The Blue Jackets begin a four-game road trip Wednesday in Anaheim...Edmonton visits St. Louis also on Wednesday...Columbus right winger David Vyborny left the game in the second period with a groin injury.
