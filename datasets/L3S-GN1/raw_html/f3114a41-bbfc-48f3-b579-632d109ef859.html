Moon Learns From His Mistake


By: Ryan McNeill   Last Updated: 1/14/08 11:15 AM ET | 105 times read





Adjust font size:

It seemed as if each of the 19,800 fans packed into the Air Canada Centre on Sunday simultaneously gasped when Jamario Moon made an ill-advised rookie blunder in the dying seconds of regulation.

With the Raptors up by four points and less than 16 seconds left in regulation Moon made what could have been a costly mistake when he fouled Travis Outlaw during a three-point attempt. Outlaw nailed the three and then calmly walked to the charity stripe and hit his free throw to push the game into overtime.



"Your hoping that doesn't happen because we were up four," an unusually stoic Sam Mitchell told reporters. "He's a young player and your hoping he understands in that situation a three doesn't beat you. But I've done something like that before in my career. The thing I remember the most is the coach didn't say a word to me because he knew I understood what I just did. I was proud of my players because nobody got on him (Moon), nobody said a word to him, everyone just patted him on the back and kept him uplifted. He came back and made a couple of blocks in that last overtime and he was able to be on the floor and got some key rebounds for us."



This is a far cry form the usually cranky and ornery Mitchell that players witness and reporters experience. Even Moon expected to get an ear full from his coach and was shocked when he didn't get blasted for his miscue.



"I didn't want to walk back to the bench," a sheepish Moon admitted after the game. "But you know what? Coach didn't even chew me out. He just said, 'keep your head in there. You need to keep playing.' So that felt good."



The decision not to chew out Moon paid-off big dividends immediately as Moon's impact in the second overtime was a big reason why Toronto escaped with a win. Moon swatted two shots in the second overtime which helped give his teammates an emotional boost when they were physically drained.



"They just wanted me to keep playing and keep being aggressive," Moon added. "Just because I fouled him they didn't want me to stop contesting shots. They wanted me to keep doing what I do. If they come in with a weak little lay-up they want me to get it out of there. I think I did a good job of that."



It's easy to pat Moon on the back now that he made a positive impact on the reset of the game but Mitchell explained he had no intention in tearing a strip off his rookie regardless of how the game ended.



"Even if we ended up losing that game I still would have not said a word to him because he had a lot to do with where we were today and everyone is going to make mistakes. You cannot just love him when they do good, you have to correct them and push them but we do not throw players away. We have some young players and they made some mistakes but like I tell our guys all the time, it's my job to correct the mistakes and my job to get on people and for them to lift each other up."



It was as if the players listened in to Mitchell's post game press conference because every player in the Raptors locker room did a great job of lifting Moon up and refused to place blame on the rookie.



Jose Calderon acted like a veteran leader and pulled Moon aside at the time and offered up some words of assurance.



"I talked to him and I was trying to tell him 'just play basketball, you know how to play and it doesn't matter if you make one mistake. We are here to help you, we are a team' You have to support everybody."



Chris Bosh wasn't too talkative at the time but was did the whole strong silent type role he personifies.



"When that happened I elected not to say anything, I was too angry," Bosh joked with reporters while laughing. "Naw, there isn't anything I was going to tell him that he didn't know. We just had to pick him up because he was going to be down on himself already.  He came out in overtime and he played well for us."



Toronto is a young team and over the past few weeks Mitchell has been preaching about the need to close out games and start doing the little things. Following the game Mitchell talked like a proud father when he talked about his team maturing.



"I think with a win today, our players grew up."



Moon was asked about this and agreed with his coaches thoughts and felt they applied directly to him today.



"I think that one play, the foul, I think I grew up from the time I made the play until the end of the game. I think it showed me a lot. Just because you make a bad play you need to let it go. The game continues. You need to continue to make good plays to help the team get a win."



Over the Raptors last three games they have done a lot of growing as individual players and as a team. They're doing a better job of crashing the glass, their defensive intensity has picked up and slowly they are doing better at closing out quarters.



This afternoon they learned how to help a teammate grow while also growing themselves as teammates.
