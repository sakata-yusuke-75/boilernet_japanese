No promises made as Smit, De Villiers meet

16 January 2008, 11:22

New Springbok coach Peter de Villiers is a winner whose only agenda is sustaining the success of the world champions, says the man who lifted the Webb Ellis Cup on that enchanted evening in Paris.



John Smit, the World Cup-winning captain, told The Pretoria News: "The guy wants what is best for the Springboks. If you are a South African rugby player or supporter you have to be encouraged by that. He's had success at national under-19 and under-21 level. His track record is one of producing winning teams."



Smit, reacting to reports that senior World Cup Springboks were demanding a meeting with De Villiers to allay fears about their future, said no player had the right to ask for any selection guarantee. He included himself in this category.



"Yes we won the World Cup, but no player in that World Cup squad is bigger than the game. No player has ownership of the Springbok jersey. It is earned through performance.



"I said it before we played Wales (last November) that we have to move on from the World Cup success. I'd like to think we already have," said Smit.



"As far as I am concerned nothing has changed: If a player consistently performs he makes it hard for the selectors not to pick him. No player should be given preferential treatment. It is about how he is performing in 2008 that matters.



"I was fortunate to chat with Peter and I'm as optimistic about the Boks in 2008 as I was about the Boks in 2007. Whether I am picked for the Springboks in 2008 or not I am comfortable the future is bright for the Springboks. I don't share the view that all is doom and gloom and I think those opinions are ill-informed and unfair towards the new coach."



Smit,who had never before met De Villiers, said he was impressed by the coach's passion, knowledge and desire to be successful.



"We spoke about a range of things, but primarily it was an opportunity for me to meet him and vice versa. He made me no promise about selection or captaincy and I certainly would never have had the audacity to ask for one. My desire is to play for the Boks if the coach and selectors deem me to be good enough. Playing abroad has not lessened my passion for the Springboks.



"I find it disturbing that there is so much negativity about the future of the game in South Africa because we have a fantastic pool of players. There is no reason why the Boks should not be successful under Peter de Villiers."



De Villiers, hesitant to elaborate on the specifics of the meeting, acknowledged the value of engaging face to face with Smit.



"He is the guy who captained the Springboks to the World Cup title. He has led the team with great integrity for the past four years and as I had never spoken to him before it was an ideal opportunity to say hello and get a first hand feel as to how he views the future. In the next few months I will be chatting to other role players in the game, but for now it made sense to speak to the guy who has been the heartbeat of the squad for the past four years.



"It was informative and thoroughly enjoyable."



De Villiers was emphatic he had struck no deal with Smit or anyone regarding the composition of his first Bok team or the make-up of the leadership within the squad.



"I have been consistent that if a player performs well enough he will be selected. I have also been consistent in my praise for what the World Cup Boks achieved in France and acknowledge that many of them still have an international future if they play well," said De Villiers.



"There has been mixed reaction to my appointment and because of this there has been varying interpretations of what I've said since being made Bok coach. Yet there is nothing to read into what I have stated, outside of the obvious, which is every player has an equal chance of making the squad, regardless of colour, experience or age. I want to win and to win you need your best team out there."



Smit, who injured knee ligaments at training on New Year's eve, returns to France in the next week, hopeful of playing again within a month.
