'No exceptions to settlement freeze'

Sat, 29 Dec 2007 17:50:54

Israeli Har Homa settlement in the West Bank
The Palestinian National Authority (PNA) will not accept any exceptions to Israeli settlement freeze in the West Bank, a top official says.



"The obligation before Israel is that the settlement activities, including the natural growth, come to a halt. So, there must be settlement or no settlement... there is no room for exceptions," Palestinian negotiator Saeb Erekat said on Saturday.



The Palestinians were angered by Israeli tenders to expand Har Homa settlement in the disputed east al-Quds (Jerusalem).



The tenders came after Annapolis gathering which set to restart Israeli-Palestinian peace talks.



Press reports say Israel was going to make concessions on settlement activities in the West Bank ahead of US President George Bush's visit to the region in January.



"Excluding Abu Ghneim Mountain (Har Homa) from the freeze can't be accepted," Xinhua quoted Erekat as saying.



The two sides reopened their peace talks on Dec. 12 as a result of Annapolis but have failed to yield any tangible progress.
