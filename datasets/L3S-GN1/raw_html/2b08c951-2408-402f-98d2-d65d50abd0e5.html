Undeck the halls and mulch the tree

Last Updated: Thursday, December 27, 2007 | 10:56 AM CT

CBC News

For Winnipeggers wanting to recycle their live Christmas trees instead of throwing them in the garbage, the city has drop-off locations for mulching.

Nine fenced depots are open across the city until Jan. 20 for trees that have lost their holiday lustre.

These depots are at Kildonan Park, Whittier Park, Assiniboine Park, St. Vital Park, King's Park, Kilcona Park, the St. James Civic Centre, the Pan Am Pool parking lot and the corner of Chrislind Street and Ravelston Avenue in Transcona.

The trees are turned into wood chips, which are then available for free from all depots.

Trees must be cleared of decorations before they can be chipped.

The National Christmas Tree Association says 93 per cent of consumers who use a live Christmas tree recycle it in some type of community program.

The group, which represents tree growers in Canada and the U.S., notes real trees are a renewable, recyclable resource while artificial trees contain non-biodegradable plastics and possible metal toxins such as lead.

For each real Christmas tree harvested, the association says up to three new seedlings are planted in its place, depending on farm size and field rotation. This year, Christmas tree farmers in North America planted more than 60 million new seedlings, the organization said.

The top selling Christmas trees are Balsam fir, Douglas fir, Fraser fir, Noble fir, Scotch pine, Virginia pine and white pine.
